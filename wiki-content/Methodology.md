# Methodology: How This Book Was Created

## Overview

This 200-page book was created in an intensive **2-day sprint** using AI-assisted research. Here's exactly how it was done.

## The Tools

### Primary: Google Antigravity
- **What**: Google's new AI coding assistant (free tier)
- **Powered by**: Gemini 3.0 Thinking
- **Role**: Primary research engine, content generation, pattern recognition
- **Cost**: $0 (free tier)

### Secondary: Anthropic Claude
- **Role**: Verification, counter-argument generation, fact-checking
- **Cost**: $0 (free tier)

### Supporting Tools
- **Quarto**: Book publishing platform
- **GitHub**: Version control and hosting
- **AI Image Generation**: Infographics and diagrams

## The 2-Day Workflow

### Day 1: Research & Framework Discovery

**Morning Session (9 AM - 12 PM)**
- Started conversational research in Google Antigravity
- Initial prompts focused on theological foundations:
  - "Explain the concept of *takfir* in Islamic jurisprudence, citing primary sources"
  - "Compare ISIS rhetoric to Spanish Inquisition rhetoric - structural similarities?"
  - "Analyze Surah 9:5 in classical Arabic and explain how Qutb vs medieval scholars interpreted it"
- AI analyzed texts in Arabic, Latin, Turkish without language training

**Afternoon Session (1 PM - 5 PM)**
- Pattern recognition emerged from comparative analyses
- Key prompt: "I notice both movements emphasize purity. Is this universal? Check Buddhist nationalism (Myanmar) and Hindu nationalism (India)"
- The "5 Dynamics" framework crystalized through iterative questioning
- Cross-referenced with psychological research (Social Identity Theory, Moral Foundations)

**Evening Session (7 PM - 10 PM)**
- Framework validation against additional cases
- Tested against: Rwandan genocide, Christian Inquisition, secular movements
- Refined scope and definitions
- Prepared chapter outline

### Day 2: Content Generation & Refinement

**Morning Session (9 AM - 1 PM)**
- Systematic chapter creation using established framework
- Sample prompts:
  - "Write a chapter analyzing the Mongol invasion of Baghdad (1258) through collective trauma theory, cite historical chronicles"
  - "Generate comparative analysis: how did Christianity, Buddhism, and Islam each weaponize purity narratives throughout history?"
- Generated 10-15 chapter drafts
- AI handled multi-language source analysis

**Afternoon Session (2 PM - 6 PM)**
- Intensive refinement cycle
- Fact-checking:
  - Verified dates, events, theological claims
  - Cross-checked AI citations against real sources
  - Removed hallucinations (invented sources, merged events)
- Prose improvement:
  - Made language less generic
  - Added narrative voice
  - Inserted "counterintuitive insight" boxes (human contribution)

**Evening Session (7 PM - 11 PM)**
- Structural assembly
- Compiled chapters into coherent narrative
- Added transitions between sections
- Refined introduction and conclusion
- Created final structure
- Generated infographics using AI image tools

## Prompting Strategies That Worked

### 1. Hyper-Specific Questions
❌ Bad: "Tell me about Ibn Taymiyyah"
✅ Good: "Analyze Ibn Taymiyyah's Mardin Fatwa, specifically the word *yuqātal* vs *yu'āmal* and how modern extremists misuse it"

### 2. Demand Citations
Always required: "Cite your primary sources" to enable verification

### 3. Rapid Iteration
- Never accepted first output
- Refined through follow-up questions
- Asked "What did I miss?" repeatedly

### 4. Parallel Verification
- Used Claude to fact-check Gemini outputs in real-time
- Cross-referenced between AI systems

### 5. Strategic Frameworks
- Gave AI proven analytical models to apply:
  - Social Identity Theory
  - Moral Foundations Theory
  - Kruglanski's Three Pillars
- AI excels when given structure to work within

## Quality Control

### Verification Process
1. **Citation Check**: Verified major sources exist and are correctly described
2. **Fact Check**: Cross-referenced historical dates, events, figures
3. **Theological Check**: Compared AI's theological interpretations against established scholarship
4. **Logic Check**: Ensured arguments followed coherently
5. **Hallucination Removal**: Deleted invented sources or merged events

### Known Limitations
- **Breadth vs Depth**: AI-curated sources may miss niche scholarship
- **Potential Errors**: Some claims may reflect AI synthesis rather than scholarly consensus
- **Verification Incomplete**: Not every citation was manually verified (time constraint)
- **Experimental**: This is testing what's possible, not claiming perfection
